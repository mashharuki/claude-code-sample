# Delivery & Submission Guide

Load when the builder is preparing for a live stage pitch (Demo Day, Breakpoint, on-stage Colosseum, public event) OR an async hackathon submission (Colosseum / Grizzlython / written + video format).

---

## LIVE STAGE PITCHING

### Pre-show checklist
- Check the stage: presenter monitor? Mic type (handheld, lapel, headset, podium)? Clicker? Online platform settings?
- Test all embedded videos BEFORE going live, external videos break on stage. If a video fails mid-pitch: don't react, don't complain, keep speaking.
- Walk the stage area early. Visualize yourself presenting before the event starts.

### Mic control
- Optimal: 10cm from mouth, slightly angled.
- New to mics? Stick it to your chin and keep it still. Moving mic = volume goes up and down = audience disconnects.

### Body and movement
- Imagine a 2m² box around you. That's your performance zone. Stay anchored.
- Move with intention: 2-3 steps, pause, deliver a key line, return. Deliberate movement = confidence. Nervous pacing = distraction.
- Never turn your back to read your slides. Either memorize your pitch or use a presenter monitor.

### Eye contact
- Create 3 imaginary points in front of you: right, center, left, slightly above eye level.
- Scan across them as you speak. Everyone feels you're looking at them.
- You don't get thrown off by someone checking their phone.

### Voice and performance
- Use silence. Pause after your strongest lines. Let it land.
- Use your face: more energy on the problem, genuine conviction on the solution.
- Your pitch is a performance. The person on stage is a character representing your whole team's work. It's not about ego, it's about doing justice to what you built.

### What to do if something breaks
- Video fails → keep speaking. Describe what the demo would have shown. Move on. Don't apologize.
- Slide doesn't advance → click again calmly, or skip to the next one. Don't say "sorry, technical issue."
- You forget a line → bridge with a transition phrase ("And that brings us to…"). Never freeze and apologize.
- Mic dies → speak louder, keep going. The room will adjust.

The unspoken rule: judges respect composure over polish. A flawless pitch with no presence loses to a slightly imperfect pitch with conviction.

## ASYNC HACKATHON SUBMISSIONS

Different constraints from a live pitch, judges review at their own pace, demo video matters more, and clarity beats performance.

### 1. Time your full submission
Give your complete submission, deck + video + description, to someone unfamiliar with it. If it takes more than 5–7 minutes to go through everything, cut it. Ideally a judge understands what you built and why it matters in 4 minutes flat.

### 2. Record yourself and watch it back
Most builders never watch their own pitch. Do it. You'll catch filler words, trust-killing language, pacing issues, and dead slides in 5 minutes. Share it with others before submitting, iterate in public.

### 3. Publish your pitch on your legends.fun product page
Make everything accessible. Your pitch shouldn't live only inside a submission form, put it where the ecosystem can see it, upvote it, and rally behind it.

### 4. Your demo video must stand alone
Some judges watch the demo before reading anything else. If they can't understand what's happening in your demo without context, they won't pay much attention to the pitch. No music, no external voiceover, no rushed scrolling. Record your screen, voiceover yourself, show the wow moment first.

### 5. Explain what it does before how it works
Use case before tech. Jargon is fine in small doses, but if you can't explain the use case simply before getting technical, judges will disengage. Simple first, depth second.

### 6. Show you care
Passion, conviction, and curiosity are visible even in a recorded pitch. A polished submission communicates that you take this seriously. Judges can hear the difference between a team that rehearsed 3 times and one that rehearsed 30 times. The 30-time team wins.

## SUBMISSION CHECKLIST (Colosseum / Grizzlython format)

Before hitting submit:

- [ ] Deck is a single PDF, demo video embedded inside (not linked)
- [ ] Standalone demo video uploaded separately too (judges may watch it first)
- [ ] Written description matches the deck's framing, no contradictions
- [ ] Live link to the product (mainnet preferred, devnet acceptable for early stage)
- [ ] GitHub link (clean README, builders judge code quality at this stage)
- [ ] legends.fun product page live with the pitch published
- [ ] Twitter/X account active, judges check social proof
- [ ] Any partner / mentor / KOL who can publicly back you on CT before judging window closes
- [ ] Total review time tested: under 7 minutes for a stranger to "get it"

---

## ONE LAST RULE

Every winning Solana submission shipped before submission deadline AND practiced the pitch 20+ times before recording. Builders who shipped on the last day and recorded their pitch in one take in their bedroom rarely win. The 1% acceptance rate at Colosseum is real. Treat the submission as the most important deliverable of the hackathon.
