# Pitch Script & Language Rules

Use this during Step 4: writing the spoken narrative, and any time you need to scrub a script for trust-killing language.

---

## SCRIPT STRUCTURE

The script should:

- **Open with a hook:** a story, a sharp stat, a provocation, or a surprising comparison that creates tension
- **Follow the storytelling structure:** Why (user in pain) → Struggle (consequence of the problem) → Resolution (your solution arrives)
- **Use natural transitions** between slides, never "next slide." Examples:
  - *"And that's exactly what we're solving."*
  - *"And that's when we decided to build [X]."*
  - *"So how do we actually make money?"*
  - *"Here's the proof we're not just talking."*
- **Use present tense everywhere except the roadmap.** Not "we're trying to build", "we built." Not "we hope to achieve", "we deliver."
- **Web3-native language where it fits naturally** (TVL, on-chain, composability), never forced
- **End with a clear, memorable ask**: a statement, not a question

The pitch is a logical AND emotional funnel, not a checklist.

## LANGUAGE AUDIT (run this on every transcript)

This is the silent killer. Every time I coach someone, this comes up. Scan the full transcript and flag every instance of:

### 🚫 CATEGORY 1: Uncertainty verbs
*Signal you don't know what you're doing.*

- "trying to" → replace with "doing" or the direct action verb
- "hoping to" → replace with "on track to" or "planning to"
- "we imagine" → replace with "our vision is" or "we're building toward"
- "attempting to" → replace with the present tense action

> ❌ "So what we're trying to do is solve liquidity fragmentation"
> ✅ "We solve liquidity fragmentation"

### 🚫 CATEGORY 2: Conditional forms
*Imply uncertainty, destroy trust.*

- would / should / could / might → all must be replaced
- "This would target enterprise clients" → "We target enterprise clients"
- "This might be a $1B market" → "This is a $1B market based on [data]"
- "We could scale this model" → "We scale this model through [strategy]"
- "Users should love this feature" → "Users love this feature, we've seen [result]"

> ❌ "We could potentially reach 10K users by Q2"
> ✅ "We reach 10K users by Q2" (or "We're on track for 10K users by Q2" if roadmap)

### 🚫 CATEGORY 3: Future tense outside the roadmap
*Future tense is ONLY acceptable on the roadmap slide.*

- Everywhere else: present tense only, even for things not fully live yet
- "We will provide" → "We provide"
- "We are going to launch" → "We launch" or "We're live"
- "We project X revenue" → "We're on track for X revenue" or "This generates X revenue"

> ❌ "We project $1.3M in revenue"
> ✅ "This generates $1.3M in revenue" or "We're on track for $1.3M ARR"

### 🚫 CATEGORY 4: Hedging words on numbers
*"More than $2M" sounds like you don't really know your numbers.*

- "More than X" → be precise: "$2.05M"
- "Around 10,000 users" → "10,247 users"
- "Roughly 30%" → "31%"

Investors notice hedging language everywhere, not just in verbs. Specificity = confidence.

---

## FORMAT FOR FLAGGING ISSUES

> 🚫 **[Slide context or timestamp]:** "exact words used"
> ✅ **Fix:** "rewritten version"

If a transcript has 3+ violations in the same category, name it as a pattern:
> "You're using conditional forms throughout, this is quietly destroying trust in everything you say. Here are the 4 instances to fix..."

If the transcript is clean on language, acknowledge it explicitly:
> "Language is sharp, present tense, confident, no conditionals. That's rare."

## CONCRETE REWRITE EXAMPLES

Use these as templates. The pattern is: identify the weak version → write the better version → make the gap visible.

> ❌ **Current:** "The DeFi market is huge and growing."
> ✅ **Better:** "Solana DEXs process $2B+ in daily volume. 40% flows through aggregators with zero routing transparency, that's $800M/year in value the user never sees. That's the problem we solve."

> ❌ **Current:** "We're trying to bring better UX to Solana wallets."
> ✅ **Better:** "We give Solana wallets the UX of Robinhood. One tap, one transaction, no extension popups. We've shipped this to 12K wallets in 60 days."

> ❌ **Current:** "We could potentially become the leading aggregator on Solana."
> ✅ **Better:** "We're the #3 aggregator on Solana by daily volume after Jupiter and Titan. We grew from 0 to that position in 5 months."

Apply this to every line that hedges, every claim that's vague, every number that's rounded.

## STORYTELLING FRAMEWORKS YOU CAN PLUG IN

**The Why → Struggle → Resolution arc** (default, works for any pitch)
- Why: the user, the situation, what they're trying to do
- Struggle: the wall they hit, the cost of that wall
- Resolution: your product, the moment of relief

**The Tokamai hook structure** (for technical / infra products)
- State the obvious first → "Solana dominates crypto"
- Then twist it → "But [something nobody noticed] is broken"
- Then comparison → "Where is the [Web2 equivalent] of Web3?"

**The personal origin story** (for consumer / community products)
- "I needed this myself. Here's the moment I got fed up. So I built it."
- Works only if it's true. Never fake an origin story, judges can tell.

**The market gap reveal** (for ICM / launchpad / DeFi)
- "Here's how much money flows through [X] every day."
- "Here's who captures it."
- "Here's why that's broken."
- "Here's how we fix it."

## CHECKLIST BEFORE DECLARING THE SCRIPT DONE

- [ ] No banned words anywhere except the roadmap slide
- [ ] Every slide has a transition sentence into the next one (never "next slide")
- [ ] Hook lands in the first 15 seconds
- [ ] Numbers are specific, not rounded
- [ ] CTA is a statement, not a question
- [ ] Closes with the one thing you want them to remember
- [ ] Total spoken time matches the actual time limit (script + read aloud with timer, not estimate)
